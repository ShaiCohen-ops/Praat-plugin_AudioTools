Adaptive Grain Cloud Synthesis: A Technical Overview

This Praat script performs spectral-morphological granular synthesis with intelligent grain sorting, transforming speech into compositionally organized sonic textures.

Core Process

The script fragments your input speech into discrete grains (micro-samples), analyzes their spectral content, then reconstructs them in a perceptually ordered sequence—creating a journey from dark (bass-heavy) to bright (treble-rich) timbres, or vice versa.

Technical Operations

1. Grain Extraction & Duration Control

Grains are extracted using user-defined envelope shapes (rectangular, triangular, or parabolic windows)

Duration modes:
- Fixed: uniform grain length
- Random: stochastic variation within specified bounds (±50ms default), bounded to 30-200% of base duration

2. Spectral Analysis & Classification

Each grain undergoes FFT analysis to calculate its spectral centroid (center of gravity in Hz)

This centroid value becomes the grain's "brightness" metric:
- Dark grains: centroid < 800 Hz (vowels, low formants, bass resonances)
- Bright grains: centroid > 1500 Hz (fricatives, sibilants, high harmonics)

3. Spectral Exaggeration (Optional)

The script can amplify timbral differences through frequency-selective filtering:
- Bright grains: high-pass emphasis above 1000 Hz, centroid multiplied by 1.3
- Dark grains: low-pass emphasis below 800 Hz, centroid multiplied by 0.7
- Intensity levels: Subtle (1.2×), Moderate (1.5×), Strong (2.0×) spectral boost

4. Adaptive Pitch Shifting

Grains receive content-aware transposition:
- High-centroid grains: +0.5 semitones ± scatter
- Low-centroid grains: -0.3 semitones ± scatter
- Enhances the perceptual separation between timbral categories

5. Bubble Sort by Brightness

A simple comparison algorithm reorganizes grains by ascending or descending centroid values, creating a timbral gradient rather than temporal sequence.

6. Concatenation with Temporal Gaps

Sorted grains are reassembled with optional silence intervals (default 50ms), allowing each grain's timbre to be perceived discretely rather than masked by overlap.

7. Dynamic Amplitude Scaling

- Bright grains: 0.35 amplitude (naturally more perceptually prominent)
- Dark grains: 0.25 amplitude (preventing muddiness)
- Duration-based compensation: short grains boosted 1.1×, long grains reduced 0.9×
- Final limiting at 0.9 peak to preserve dynamic range

Compositional Implications

From speech, this process:
- Deconstructs phonemic identity: vowels and consonants become timbral gestures divorced from linguistic meaning
- Creates spectral narratives: the sorted sequence forms an arc from guttural to whispered, or vice versa
- Reveals hidden formants: spectral exaggeration makes subtle resonances sculptural
- Generates rhythmic-timbral counterpoint: gap insertion creates pulsed textures where rhythm is timbre-dependent

The result oscillates between acousmatic abstraction (unrecognizable source) and phonetic residue (ghostly speech fragments), with the spectral journey providing formal coherence independent of the original speech's temporal structure.

Duration statistics and brightness range are logged, providing compositional metadata for multi-layer workflows or algorithmic post-processing.
