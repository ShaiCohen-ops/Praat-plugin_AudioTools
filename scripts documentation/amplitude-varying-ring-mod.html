What this does

Praat script.

Process Description: This script applies a sophisticated form of Ring Modulation (AM synthesis) to the selected sound. It uses the original sound as the input signal and multiplies it by a complex carrier wave that simultaneously features: A frequency sweep (carrier frequency increases over time). A slow, periodic amplitude modulation (the entire effect's volume rises and falls). This technique results in a dynamic, metallic, or robotic timbre that is unstable both in pitch and volume over its duration.

Technical details: This Praat script implements a dual-modulation ring modulator combining frequency-swept carrier oscillation with sinusoidal amplitude variation, creating complex time-varying spectral transformations through nested nonlinear operations. The algorithm applies the compound formula output[n] = input[n] × sin(2π × carrier_freq × t^exponent / exponent) × (amp_center + amp_depth × sin(2π × amp_rate × t)), where three simultaneous processes reshape the signal: first, ring modulation multiplies the input by a sinusoidal carrier creating sum and difference frequencies at f_input ± f_carrier for each spectral component; second, the carrier frequency undergoes power-law sweeping through the time-exponentiation term t^exponent, where the exponent parameter controls acceleration characteristics—linear sweep (exponent=1) produces constant frequency increase, quadratic sweep (exponent=2, default) creates accelerating upward glide with instantaneous frequency f(t) = carrier_freq × t^(exponent-1), and higher exponents generate increasingly rapid frequency acceleration toward the end of the signal. The division by exponent in the phase accumulation term (t^exponent / exponent) ensures correct phase integration, as the derivative of this expression yields the desired instantaneous frequency. Third, the entire ring-modulated signal undergoes amplitude modulation at a separate rate (default 3 Hz), with the modulation depth controlled by center and depth parameters that define the amplitude envelope range: amp_center - amp_depth to amp_center + amp_depth (defaulting to 0-1.0 range). This creates a tremolo effect superimposed on the ring modulation, where the sideband intensities fluctuate periodically, generating additional spectral complexity through the interaction of carrier sidebands with amplitude modulation sidebands—effectively a four-sideband structure around each input frequency component (f_input, f_input ± f_carrier(t), f_input ± f_amp_rate, f_input ± f_carrier(t) ± f_amp_rate). The frequency sweep creates dramatic timbral evolution from relatively low-frequency metallic coloration at the onset to increasingly dense, high-frequency textures as the carrier frequency accelerates, with the power-law exponent determining whether this evolution is gradual (low exponents) or explosive (high exponents). The amplitude modulation adds rhythmic pulsation and prevents static timbre, creating breathing, wavering effects characteristic of vintage analog ring modulators with voltage-controlled carrier oscillators. Combined, these three processes—ring modulation, frequency sweeping, and amplitude variation—generate rich, evolving metallic and robotic timbres with time-varying spectral density, suitable for science fiction sound effects, experimental vocal processing, dramatic textural transformations, and abstract sound design where multiple simultaneous modulation processes create complex, non-stationary spectra that continuously evolve throughout the signal duration while maintaining the characteristic inharmonic, metallic quality of ring modulation artifacts.

Quick start

  1. In Praat, select a Sound object.
  2. Run script… → AMPLITUDE-VARYING RING MOD.praat.
  3. Set parameters and click OK.
  4. Output object appears (see Outputs).
